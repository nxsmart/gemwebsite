01

Air Quality & Pediatric Asthma Rates in Urban Counties

Question: Is there a statistically significant association between annual PM2.5 concentrations and pediatric asthma hospitalizations across urban U.S. counties?

Methods & Tools: R (tidyverse, ggplot2), EPA AQS data, CDC BRFSS data, linear regression, choropleth mapping with ggmap

Key Takeaway: Found a statistically significant positive association (p < 0.01) between PM2.5 and hospitalization rates; counties above the NAAQS threshold showed 23% higher asthma admission rates on average.

R Air Quality Regression
02

Lead Exposure Risk Mapping — Baltimore Neighborhoods

Question: Which Baltimore neighborhoods face compounded lead exposure risk from both housing age and proximity to industrial sites?

Methods & Tools: QGIS, Python (geopandas, folium), Baltimore Open Data Portal, spatial join analysis, composite risk scoring

Key Takeaway: Identified seven high-priority census tracts where pre-1940 housing stock overlaps within 500m of legacy industrial zones, providing a tiered intervention map for hypothetical public health resource allocation.

Python GIS Spatial Analysis
03

PFAS Contamination in Public Water Systems — Statistical Overview

Question: What patterns exist in PFAS detection across U.S. public water systems, and are rural systems disproportionately under-tested?

Methods & Tools: R (dplyr, ggplot2), EPA UCMR 5 dataset, chi-square tests, descriptive statistics, comparative visualization

Key Takeaway: Analysis revealed that small, rural community water systems were tested at significantly lower rates; among tested systems, PFOA/PFOS co-occurrence was more common in agricultural regions, suggesting runoff as a contributing pathway.

R PFAS Public Health
04

Heat Island Vulnerability Index — Course Capstone

Question: Can a composite vulnerability index combining land surface temperature, tree canopy coverage, and socioeconomic indicators predict heat-related illness burden?

Methods & Tools: NASA Landsat imagery (Google Earth Engine), Census ACS data, R (sf, terra), principal component analysis, index construction

Key Takeaway: Developed a five-component Heat Vulnerability Index; PCA revealed that tree canopy deficit and impervious surface coverage explained 61% of variance in surface temperature anomalies — suggesting targeted greening as a high-leverage intervention.

R Remote Sensing PCA
